Grade 11 General Mathematics | Second Quarter Overview

Subject: General Mathematics

Grade Level: 11

Quarter: Second Quarter, SY 2025-2026

Class Schedule: 4 sessions per week, 1 hour (60 minutes) per session

Performance Standards

By the end of the quarter, the learners are able to present solutions to real-life problems that require measurements and conversions (such as designing a garden, cooking recipes, or building a model). Learners apply trigonometry and geometric concepts to estimate measurements and construct three-dimensional objects, understanding their shapes and volumes for practical uses like product packaging, furniture, and construction.

Performance Tasks Overview

Performance Task #1: The Green Campus Initiative - Design Phase

In groups of 4-5, students will be assigned a specific, underutilized area of the school campus. Their task is to create a detailed landscape design proposal for this area, focusing on accurate measurement, scaled drawings, and calculations of perimeter and area.

Performance Task #2: The Green Campus Initiative - Build & Budget Phase

Continuing from PT #1, the same groups will now turn their design into a practical project plan. This phase focuses on volume, surface area, trigonometry, and creating a detailed budget by researching the real-world costs of materials.

Performance Task #1: The Green Campus Initiative - Design Phase

Total Points: 30

I. Detailed Instructions for Students

Project Title: The Green Campus Initiative - Design Phase

Introduction: Welcome, junior landscape architects! Your group has been selected by the school administration to help beautify our campus. Your task is to create a practical and beautiful design proposal for an underutilized space. This project will test your skills in measurement, geometry, and scale drawing.

Your Goal: To produce a short, professional report that proposes a new landscape design, including a scaled drawing, precise calculations, and a clear justification for your choices.

Step-by-Step Instructions:
  1. Site Inspection & Measurement (The "Before"): Visit your assigned site. Accurately measure the overall dimensions and note any existing features. Create a rough sketch with all measurements labeled.
  2. Brainstorming & Design (The "Vision"): As a group, brainstorm how you want to transform the space. Consider function, aesthetics, and elements like lawns, pathways, and flower beds.
  3. Creating the Scaled Blueprint (The "Plan"): On graph paper, create a formal, scaled drawing of your proposed design. State the scale clearly (e.g., 1 cm = 0.5 m). Label everything and include a legend.
  4. Mathematical Calculations (The "Proof"): On a separate sheet, present the following calculations with formulas and step-by-step solutions:
    • Total perimeter and total area of the entire space.
    • Area of each individual element in your design (e.g., lawn, pathway).
    • Perimeter/circumference of relevant elements (e.g., border for a flower bed).
  5. Design Justification (The "Why"): Write a short paragraph (3-5 sentences) explaining your design choices and how they improve the space.
  6. Final Report Assembly: Compile a neat report with a Title Page, the Scaled Blueprint, the Calculation Sheet, and the Design Justification.

II. Timeline of Activities

  • Cycle 2, Day 2: Task Introduction & Site Inspection.
  • Cycle 2, Day 3: Measurement & Planning.
  • Cycle 3, Day 3 & 4: In-Class Work Sessions for design, blueprinting, and calculations.
  • Cycle 4, Day 1: Finalization & Submission.

Performance Task #2: The Green Campus Initiative - Build & Budget Phase

Total Points: 32

I. Detailed Instructions for Students

Project Title: The Green Campus Initiative - Build & Budget Phase

Introduction: Congratulations on completing the design phase! Now it's time to see if your design is practical and affordable. You will calculate materials needed, solve a real-world trigonometry problem, and create a complete budget.

Your Goal: To produce a project folder containing a calculations report and a budget spreadsheet that details the costs and materials required to bring your landscape design to life.

Step-by-Step Instructions:
  1. Volume Calculations (The "Materials"): Using your PT#1 design, calculate the volume of materials needed (e.g., cubic meters of soil for a raised garden bed). Show your formula (V = Area of Base × height) and calculations.
  2. 3D Object Design & Calculations (The "Feature"): Design one simple 3D object for your space (e.g., a rectangular planter box). Create a sketch with dimensions. Calculate its Surface Area (for painting/finishing) and its Volume/Capacity. Show all formulas and calculations.
  3. Trigonometry Application (The "Site Problem"): Solve one practical trigonometry problem relevant to your site (e.g., calculate the height of a nearby tree). You must measure a distance on the ground and an angle of elevation. Provide a diagram, your measurements, and the full calculation using SOH-CAH-TOA.
  4. Creating the Project Budget (The "Cost"): Using a spreadsheet program, create a detailed budget. Research realistic prices for materials. Your spreadsheet must have columns for: Item, Description/Source, Quantity, Unit Cost, and Total Cost. Include a Grand Total.
  5. Final Project Folder Assembly: Compile everything into a folder containing your Calculations Report and your Budget Spreadsheet.

II. Timeline of Activities

  • Cycle 5, Day 3: Task Introduction.
  • Cycle 5, Day 4: In-Class Work Session 1 for calculations and cost research.
  • Cycle 6, Day 1: Site Check (for trigonometry problem) & Work Session 2.
  • Cycle 6, Day 2: Finalization of report and budget.
  • Cycle 6, Day 4: Submission.

Cycle 1: Foundations of Measurement and Conversion

Weekly Summary & Objectives: This cycle establishes the fundamentals of measurement. Students will learn to identify appropriate units, convert between systems, and apply these skills to practical contexts like currency, time, and temperature.

Materials Needed:
  • Teacher: Whiteboard/markers, TV/computer, rulers, meter sticks, measuring tapes, sample foreign currency bills (or images), world clock website, thermometers (Celsius/Fahrenheit).
  • Students: Notebook, pen, calculator, ruler.

Day 1: The Language of Measurement

Topic: Appropriate Metric Units and Estimating Lengths.

determine and apply the appropriate metric units... (T); estimate and measure lengths and distances (A).

Engage (5 mins): Show images of very small (ant) and very large (galaxy) objects. Ask: "How would you measure these? What units would you use?"
Explore (25 mins / Outdoor Activity): "Outdoor Estimation Challenge". In groups, students visit pre-identified stations outdoors. They first estimate the length/height of objects (e.g., gate width, basketball hoop height) and choose an appropriate metric unit (mm, cm, m). Then, they use measuring tools to find the actual measurement and compare.
Explain (15 mins): Debrief the activity. Discuss the metric system, appropriate units, and the importance of estimation.
Elaborate (10 mins): Students work on practice problems converting between meters, centimeters, and millimeters.
Evaluate (5 mins): Exit Ticket: "What is a more appropriate unit to measure the thickness of your phone: millimeters or meters? Why?"

Day 2: Conversions Across Systems

Topic: Unit Conversion (Dimensional Analysis), Currency, Time, and Temperature.

convert measurements with different units... (A); solve problems involving the conversion of currencies (T); apply time and temperature conversions... (T).

Engage (5 mins): Ask: "If a souvenir in Japan costs 1,500 Yen, is that expensive?" Show a real-time currency exchange rate.
Explore (10 mins): In pairs, students figure out how to convert an amount between two currencies using the displayed rates.
Explain (25 mins): Formalize dimensional analysis for converting units (e.g., km/hr to m/s). Apply the same logic to currency, time zone, and temperature conversions (Celsius to Fahrenheit).
Elaborate (15 mins): Students solve mixed word problems involving multi-step conversions.
Evaluate (5 mins): Quick poll: "Convert 50 US Dollars to Philippine Pesos using today's exchange rate."

Day 3: 2D Geometry of Polygons

Topic: Perimeter and Area of Squares, Rectangles, and Triangles.

calculate the area and perimeter of residential spaces... (A).

Engage (5 mins): Show a picture of a fenced-in garden. Ask: "What measurement relates to the fence? What relates to the planting space?"
Explore (15 mins): "Shape Shifters." Groups use a 2-meter rope to form a square, a long rectangle, and a triangle, discussing which shape encloses the most space.
Explain (25 mins): Formalize the formulas for perimeter and area of squares, rectangles, and triangles, including composite shapes.
Elaborate (10 mins): Students work on worksheet problems involving composite shapes.
Evaluate (5 mins): Exit Ticket: "Draw two different rectangles that both have an area of 36 sq. cm. What are their perimeters?"

Day 4: Quiz #1 & Circles

Topic: Assessment of Conversions; Circumference and Area of Circles.

(Assessment of Cycle 1, Day 1-2 competencies); calculate the area and perimeter of residential spaces (A).

Evaluate (25 mins): Quiz #1 (Individual) covering metric units and conversions.
Engage (5 mins): After the quiz, show a circular running track. Ask: "How do we measure one lap? How do we find the area inside?"
Explain (15 mins): Introduce Pi (π), radius, and diameter, and the formulas for circumference and area of circles.
Elaborate (15 mins): Solve problems involving sectors and composite shapes with circles.

Cycle 2: Advanced 2D Geometry & PT#1 Introduction

Weekly Summary & Objectives: Students will tackle more complex 2D shapes, learn to use scale drawings, and begin applying these skills by starting Performance Task #1.

Materials Needed:
  • Teacher: TV/computer, graph paper, rulers, meter sticks, measuring tapes, sample blueprints or maps.
  • Students: Notebook, pen, calculator, ruler, graph paper.

Day 1: Area of Oblique Triangles & Scale Drawings

Topic: Area of Oblique Triangles; Using a Scale on Maps/Blueprints.

illustrate the area of oblique triangles (Heron's Formula, Area=½ abSinC) (A); use a scale to interpret and compare distances... (M).

Engage (5 mins): Show a Google Maps image. Ask: "How does this small map represent such a large area?"
Explain (25 mins): Define 'scale' and demonstrate how to convert between map and real distances. Introduce formulas for the area of oblique triangles (Heron's Formula and Area = ½ abSinC).
Elaborate (25 mins): Students work on a two-part worksheet using a map of the school to find real distances and calculating areas of oblique triangles.
Evaluate (5 mins): Ask students to interpret a scale on a provided blueprint and find one real-world distance.

Day 2: Performance Task #1 Introduction

Topic: Applying 2D Geometry to Landscape Design.

(Introduction to) estimate and measure lengths... (A); calculate area and perimeter... (A); use a scale... (M).

Engage (5 mins): Show "before and after" pictures of landscaping projects.
Explore (15 mins / Outdoor Activity): "Site Inspection." Take the class outside to view the designated areas for the performance task.
Explain (20 mins): Introduce Performance Task #1: The Green Campus Initiative - Design Phase. Discuss objectives, mechanics, and rubric.
Elaborate (15 mins): Groups are formed and assigned their area. They begin brainstorming and planning their measurement strategy.
Evaluate (5 mins): Each group submits a "Plan of Action" with member names and their initial measurement plan.

Day 3: PT #1 In-Class Work Session

Topic: Group Work on Landscape Design Proposal.

(Application of) calculate the area and perimeter... (A); use a scale... (M).

Engage (5 mins): Quick check-in with each group: "What measurements do you need to take first?"
Explore/Elaborate (50 mins): Dedicated work session for PT#1. Part of the class can go outside to measure while others begin sketching. Teacher circulates as a facilitator.
Evaluate (5 mins): Teacher observes group dynamics and progress, providing formative feedback.

Day 4: Synthesis & Problem Solving

Topic: Solving Complex Problems with 2D Shapes.

(Synthesis of all Cycle 1 & 2 competencies).

Engage (5 mins): Present a complex problem: "How much would it cost to re-tile and repaint this classroom?"
Explore (15 mins): In groups, students brainstorm the steps and calculations needed to solve the problem.
Explain/Elaborate (35 mins): Teacher guides the class through solving the problem, then students work on a challenging worksheet with similar composite area and scale problems.
Evaluate (5 mins): Final check-in on PT #1 progress and clarification of submission requirements.

Cycle 3: 3D Geometry (Surface Area and Volume)

Weekly Summary & Objectives: Students will extend their understanding to 3D objects, focusing on calculating both surface area (the "wrapping") and volume (the "filling") for various solids.

Materials Needed:
  • Teacher: Geometric solids models, cardboard boxes, cans, TV/computer.
  • Students: Notebook, pen, calculator, ruler.

Day 1: Prisms and Cylinders (SA & Volume)

Topic: Calculating the Surface Area and Volume of Prisms and Cylinders.

determine the surface area of three-dimensional objects... (A); calculate the volume and capacity of three-dimensional objects (A).

Engage (5 mins): Hold up a box. Ask: "How much paper to wrap this? (Surface Area). How much sand to fill this? (Volume)."
Explore (15 mins): In groups, students examine a small box, sketch its "net," and discuss how to find the total area.
Explain (20 mins): Define surface area and volume, and derive the formulas for prisms and cylinders.
Elaborate (15 mins): Students solve problems finding both SA and volume of various prisms and cylinders.
Evaluate (5 mins): Find the surface area AND volume of a given cylinder.

Day 2: Pyramids, Cones, and Spheres (SA & Volume)

Topic: Calculating the Surface Area and Volume of Pyramids, Cones, and Spheres.

determine the surface area of... (A); calculate the volume of... (A).

Engage (5 mins): Show images of the Pyramids of Giza, an ice cream cone, and a basketball.
Explore (15 mins / Outdoor Activity): "Shadow Cones." On a sunny day, students use a meter stick and its shadow to form a right triangle, visualizing the relationship between height, radius, and slant height.
Explain (20 mins): Introduce slant height (l). Derive and explain the formulas for surface area and volume of pyramids, cones, and spheres.
Elaborate (15 mins): Students work on problems involving all three shapes.
Evaluate (5 mins): Find the volume of a pyramid given its base dimensions and height.

Day 3: Composite Solids & PT#1 Work Session

Topic: Solving Problems with Composite 3D Shapes.

(Synthesis of) determine the surface area... (A); calculate the volume... (A).

Engage (5 mins): Show a composite object like a capsule or a house with a pyramid roof.
Explain/Elaborate (25 mins): Model how to find the volume/SA of composite solids. Students then work on a worksheet.
Elaborate (25 mins): Dedicated time for groups to continue working on their PT#1 reports, focusing on scaled drawings and calculations.
Evaluate (5 mins): Teacher circulates to provide feedback on both the worksheet and PT#1 progress.

Day 4: Quiz #2 & PT#1 Finalization

Topic: Quiz on 3D Geometry; Finalizing Landscape Design Proposal.

(Assessment of all Cycle 3 competencies).

Evaluate (25 mins): Quiz #2 (Individual) on surface area and volume of all solids.
Elaborate (35 mins): After the quiz, final dedicated work session for PT#1. Groups compile reports and prepare for submission.

Cycle 4: PT#1 Submission & Introduction to Trigonometry

Weekly Summary & Objectives: This cycle begins with the submission of PT#1. The focus then shifts to introducing trigonometry, the study of triangle measurement, starting with right-angled triangles.

Materials Needed:
  • Teacher: TV/computer, scientific calculators, clinometers (can be handmade), measuring tapes.
  • Students: Notebook, pen, scientific calculator, protractor, ruler, completed PT #1 outputs.

Day 1: Performance Task #1 Submission & Review

Topic: Finalization and Submission of Landscape Design Proposal.

(Summative Assessment of) estimate and measure lengths... (A); calculate area/perimeter... (A); use a scale... (M).

Engage (5 mins): Review the submission checklist and rubric.
Elaborate (50 mins): Final checks, compilation, and submission of Performance Task #1.
Evaluate (5 mins): Groups complete a brief self-assessment of their project and teamwork.

Day 2: The Trigonometric Ratios (SOH-CAH-TOA)

Topic: Illustrating Sine, Cosine, and Tangent in a Right-Angled Triangle.

illustrate the trigonometric ratios in a right-angled triangle... (A).

Engage (5 mins): Pose the question: "Without leaving the ground, how can we find the height of our school's flagpole?"
Explore (20 mins / Outdoor Activity): "Triangle Hunt." Students find and sketch examples of right triangles in the environment, labeling hypotenuse, opposite, and adjacent sides.
Explain (20 mins): Formalize the three basic trigonometric ratios: Sine (SOH), Cosine (CAH), and Tangent (TOA).
Elaborate (10 mins): Students practice setting up the correct ratios in various right triangles.
Evaluate (5 mins): Exit Ticket: "For a given right triangle, write down the SOH-CAH-TOA ratios for angle A."

Day 3: Solving for Sides and Angles

Topic: Using Trig Ratios to Find Missing Sides and Angles in Right Triangles.

solve practical problems involving right and oblique triangles... (T).

Engage (5 mins): Revisit the flagpole problem. "What information do we need to calculate the height?"
Explain (25 mins): Model the step-by-step process for solving for a missing side (using sin/cos/tan) and a missing angle (using inverse functions).
Elaborate (25 mins): Students work on practice problems solving for missing sides and angles.
Evaluate (5 mins): Solve for x in a given right triangle problem.

Day 4: Angles of Elevation and Depression

Topic: Solving Practical Problems with Angles of Elevation and Depression.

solve practical problems involving right/oblique triangles, angles of elevation and depression... (T).

Engage (5 mins): Show pictures taken from a plane window (depression) and looking up at a building (elevation).
Explain (10 mins): Formally define angle of elevation and angle of depression.
Explore/Elaborate (40 mins / Outdoor Activity): "Mission: Measure the Flagpole." Using handmade clinometers, groups measure the distance to the flagpole and the angle of elevation to its top. Back in the classroom, they use their data and trigonometry to calculate the height.
Evaluate (5 mins): Draw a diagram and set up the equation for a simple word problem involving an angle of depression.

Cycle 5: Advanced Trigonometry & PT#2 Introduction

Weekly Summary & Objectives: This cycle expands trigonometry beyond right triangles to solve any triangle using the Sine and Cosine Laws. Students are also introduced to navigation and will begin PT#2.

Materials Needed:
  • Teacher: TV/computer, scientific calculators, compasses, maps.
  • Students: Notebook, pen, scientific calculator.

Day 1: The Sine Law and Cosine Law

Topic: Solving Oblique Triangles using the Law of Sines and Law of Cosines.

solve practical problems involving right and oblique triangles... (T).

Engage (5 mins): Present an oblique triangle. Ask: "SOH-CAH-TOA won't work here. How can we solve it?"
Explain (25 mins): Formally state the Law of Sines and Law of Cosines, explaining the conditions for using each (AAS, ASA, SSA vs. SAS, SSS).
Elaborate (25 mins): Students work through practice problems, first identifying which law to use, then solving.
Evaluate (5 mins): "Which law would you use first to solve a triangle given SAS? Why?"

Day 2: Bearings and Navigation

Topic: Using Bearings in Navigation.

solve practical problems involving... the use of bearings in navigation (T).

Engage (5 mins): Show a clip from a movie involving navigation (ship or plane). Ask: "How do they describe direction?"
Explore (20 mins / Outdoor Activity): "Campus Navigation Challenge." In groups, follow a set of instructions like "Walk 15 meters on a bearing of N 45° E" using a compass.
Explain (15 mins): Debrief the activity and formally explain how to interpret and sketch bearings.
Elaborate (15 mins): Solve word problems that combine bearings with the Sine and Cosine Laws.
Evaluate (5 mins): Sketch the path described by: "S 20° W".

Day 3: Quiz #3 & Performance Task #2 Introduction

Topic: Quiz on Trigonometry; Introduction of PT #2.

(Assessment); (Introduction to) apply volume... (T), determine surface area... (A) for PT#2.

Evaluate (25 mins): Quiz #3 (Individual) covering SOH-CAH-TOA, Sine Law, and Cosine Law.
Explain/Elaborate (35 mins): Introduce Performance Task #2: The Green Campus Initiative - Build & Budget Phase. Explain how it builds on PT #1. Go over objectives, rubric, and timeline.

Day 4: PT #2 In-Class Work Session

Topic: Group Work on Project Build & Budget.

(Application of) calculate volume... (A); apply volume in cost estimation (T); solve problems involving triangles (T).

Engage (5 mins): Groups share the 3D object they plan to design for their project.
Elaborate (50 mins): In-class work session for PT#2. Students begin core calculations (volume, SA) and research material costs online. Teacher circulates to help.
Evaluate (5 mins): Teacher checks the progress of each group's initial calculations.

Cycle 6: Application, Review, and Final Submission

Weekly Summary & Objectives: This final cycle is dedicated to applying and synthesizing all the quarter's concepts. Students will finalize their comprehensive project proposals (PT #2), review for the final exam, and submit their work.

Materials Needed:
  • Teacher: TV/computer, review materials.
  • Students: Completed PT #2 materials, notebooks, calculators.

Day 1: PT #2 Work Session & Site Check

Topic: Group Work on Project Build & Budget.

(Synthesis of all PT#2 related competencies).

Engage (5 mins): Quick check-in: "What is the most challenging calculation for your group?"
Explore (20 mins / Outdoor Activity): "Final Site Check & Problem Solving." Groups go to their site one last time to re-verify a measurement or visualize their trigonometry problem.
Elaborate (35 mins): Back in the classroom, groups continue working on their reports and budgets.

Day 2: PT #2 Finalization

Topic: Finalizing Reports and Budgets.

(Synthesis of all PT#2 related competencies).

Engage (5 mins): Show an example of a concise and professional project proposal.
Elaborate (50 mins): Final in-class work session for PT#2. Groups finalize reports, budgets, and compile everything for submission.
Evaluate (5 mins): Teacher conducts final checks on group progress.

Day 3: Quarter Review

Topic: Comprehensive Review of Second Quarter Topics.

(Review of all Second Quarter competencies).

Engage (5 mins): "Brain dump" activity where students write down every formula and concept they remember.
Explore (25 mins): "Station rotation" activity. Four stations around the room with problems from major topics (Conversions & 2D Area; SA & Volume; Right Triangle Trig; Oblique Triangle Trig).
Explain/Elaborate (25 mins): Teacher leads a discussion focusing on the most commonly missed problems from the stations.
Evaluate (5 mins): Final Q&A session.

Day 4: Performance Task #2 Submission & Quarter Wrap-Up

Topic: Final Submission and Quarter Synthesis.

(Summative Assessment of all applied competencies).

Elaborate (45 mins): Final deadline for PT#2 submission. Afterwards, facilitate a gallery walk where students can view other groups' design ideas.
Evaluate (15 mins): Facilitate a brief wrap-up discussion, connecting all topics from the quarter and highlighting the real-world utility of the skills learned.
